# Research sources (working list)

This file tracks the books/articles/indexes/archives used to build the database. It is not exhaustive yet; add to it as new sources are discovered.

## Core reference works (books / encyclopedias / bibliographies)

- Pamela Y. Dees, *A Guide to Piano Music by Women Composers, Volume One: Composers Born Before 1900*.
- Julie Anne Sadie & Rhian Samuel (eds.), *The New Grove Dictionary of Women Composers*.
- Aaron I. Cohen, *International Encyclopedia of Women Composers*.
- Karin Pendle & Melinda Boyd, *Women in Music: A Research and Information Guide*.

## Major online databases / catalogs / indexes

- IMSLP — International Music Score Library Project: https://imslp.org (public-domain scores; composer/category pages).
- Wikidata Query Service (SPARQL): https://query.wikidata.org (used to seed composer list via IMSLP IDs + metadata).
- RISM Online — Répertoire International des Sources Musicales: https://rism.info (authority data + sources).
- Donne, Women in Music: https://donne-uk.org (searchable database with birth-year and country filters).
- Boulanger Initiative Database (BID): https://www.boulangerinitiative.org/database/ (open-access database with filters; includes score access links when available).
- MuGI — “Music / Music Mediation and Gender on the Internet” (HfMT Hamburg): https://mugi.hfmt-hamburg.de
- Archiv Frau und Musik (Frankfurt): https://www.archiv-frau-musik.de (archive holdings + online list of women composers; includes repertoire lists, including piano).
- Women Composers Collection (University of Michigan Music Library Special Collections): https://www.lib.umich.edu/collections/collecting-areas/music-library-special-collections/collections/women-composers-collection (digitized scores; many via HathiTrust).
- Piano Rare Scores (PRS Libraries) — Women composers category: https://www.pianorarescores.com/library/piano-composers/women-composers/ (piano-score listings; useful for names + editions when IMSLP is incomplete).
- Library of Congress — search + notated music records: https://www.loc.gov/search/ (edition listings and many digitized scores; used for composers missing from IMSLP).

## National / specialized digital score libraries (examples; expand by country)

- Swedish Musical Heritage: https://www.swedishmusicalheritage.com (editions + searchable composer/work catalog; model for UX/search).
- Gallica (Bibliothèque nationale de France): https://gallica.bnf.fr (scores + publications; useful for French-born composers and editions published in France).
- HathiTrust Digital Library: https://www.hathitrust.org (digitized historical scores/editions, including many women-composer holdings).
- Internet Archive: https://archive.org (digitized historical scores/editions and books).
- Polona (National Library of Poland): https://polona.pl (useful for Polish editions and composers).
- Europeana: https://www.europeana.eu (cross-archive search; useful for locating digitized scores in European collections).

## Other discovery resources (useful for cross-checking)

- Furore Verlag — women composers pages: https://furore-verlag.de/en/ (publisher focused; biographies + editions).
- KVAST Repertoire Bank (ELNA / Nordic repertoire bank): https://kvast.org (searchable catalog; useful for Nordic contexts).
- The Philomel Project: https://www.philomelproject.org/repertoire-database/ (searchable database of solo/chamber works by women composers).
